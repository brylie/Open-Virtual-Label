# Niche Research Guide

Search strategies and evaluation criteria by creator type. Use these
alongside the artist's preferred niches from `outreach-preferences.md`.

---

## Podcasts

**Search queries:**

```text
"[genre] podcast"              # e.g. "meditation podcast", "ambient music podcast"
"[topic] podcast" + email
site:podbean.com OR site:buzzsprout.com "[topic]"
"indie podcast" + "[niche]"
"[topic] interview podcast"
```

**Evaluate each podcast:**

1. Listen to 1–2 episodes — is the tone a genuine fit?
2. Check current music usage: intro/outro, transitions, background
3. Note episode topics and style
4. Identify host or producer name
5. Find contact (website email preferred over social DMs)
6. Note one specific thing you genuinely appreciated

**Fit indicators:**

- Contemplative, slow-paced delivery
- Topics: mindfulness, nature, philosophy, sleep, wellness, education
- Solo host or small production — more likely to respond personally
- No music currently, or placeholder/royalty-free music

## YouTube creators

**Search queries:**

```text
"study with me" channel
"meditation" background music
"nature" documentary indie
"ambient" [topic] channel
"lo-fi" OR "focus" study content
environmental videography
```

**Evaluate each channel:**

1. Watch 2–3 recent videos — is the aesthetic a genuine match?
2. Note what music (if any) they use and whether it fits or clashes
3. Check about page and video descriptions for contact info
4. Identify one specific video where the artist's music would work
5. Note subscriber count and engagement (comments feel personal?)

**Fit indicators:**

- Nature footage, study content, meditation, documentary
- Uses no music or royalty-free library music that feels generic
- Consistent aesthetic across videos
- Engaged comments section

## Indie game developers

**Search queries:**

```text
site:itch.io "[mood/genre]" in development
"indie game" devlog atmospheric
"game jam" ambient soundtrack
site:reddit.com/r/gamedev music licensing
"pixel art" OR "hand-drawn" exploration game
```

**Evaluate each project:**

1. Read the project description and check screenshots
2. Watch trailer or gameplay video if available
3. Assess art style and mood — does the artist's music fit?
4. Check development stage (early dev is best — they haven't scored it yet)
5. Find devlog or contact page

**Fit indicators:**

- Atmospheric exploration, puzzle, or narrative genres
- Calm or melancholic art style
- No soundtrack yet, or placeholder music
- Active development (updated in last 3 months)
- Indie or solo developer (more accessible)

## Documentary and short film

**Search queries:**

```text
"independent documentary" post-production [year]
"short film" atmospheric music needed
"environmental film" indie [year]
"documentary" + "[relevant topic]" + independent
site:vimeo.com staff picks documentary
film festival [year] indie selections
```

**Evaluate each project:**

1. Read project description and director notes
2. Check trailer if available — tone, pace, subject matter
3. Note where the project is in production (post-production is ideal)
4. Find production contact (director, producer, or production email)
5. Identify the emotional register the music needs to support

**Fit indicators:**

- Nature, environment, contemplative subjects
- Slow pacing, observational approach
- Independent production (no existing music budget or composer)
- Post-production stage (actively seeking music)

## Wellness and meditation apps

**Search queries:**

```text
"meditation app" indie developer
"mindfulness app" [platform] background music
"sleep app" ambient music
"focus app" background sound
site:producthunt.com meditation app
```

**Evaluate each app:**

1. Download or try the free version if possible
2. Check what sounds/music they currently use
3. Identify whether CC music could slot in
4. Find developer contact (often in App Store developer page or website)

**Fit indicators:**

- Guided meditation, sleep, focus, or breathing apps
- Currently using generic sounds or no music
- Small indie team (accessible)
- Values around wellness align with the artist's philosophy

## Educational content creators

**Search queries:**

```text
"educational video" background music
"explainer video" ambient
"online course" relaxing background music
site:udemy.com OR site:teachable.com [topic] instructor
"tutorial" calm background music
```

**Evaluate:**

1. Watch a sample of their content — is the tone calm and focused?
2. Check what music (if any) they use — often generic royalty-free
3. Find creator contact via their course platform profile or personal website

**Fit indicators:**

- Long-form educational content where background music aids focus
- Currently using generic library music
- Audience values calm, focused learning environments

## Verifying contact information

**Priority order:**

1. Official email listed on website or about page
2. Contact form on website
3. Social media DMs (Instagram, Twitter/X, LinkedIn) — less preferred
4. LinkedIn message for professional contexts

**Never:**

- Post in YouTube comments or podcast episode comments
- Use email addresses found on unverified third-party sites
- Mass-email a list

## Scoring prospects (match.score in opportunity record)

| Score | Meaning | Action |
| ----- | ------- | ------ |
| 8–10 | Strong fit — aesthetic match, accessible, active, likely to benefit | Prioritise; draft outreach this session |
| 5–7 | Reasonable fit — some uncertainty about aesthetic or accessibility | Pursue when capacity allows |
| 1–4 | Weak fit or unlikely to respond | Skip; add to `stale` or `declined` |

Write the rationale in `match.rationale` — reference specific evidence, not
general impressions.
