# Email Templates

Structure and worked examples for licensing outreach. Every email sent must
be individually personalised — use these as structural guides, not copy-paste
templates.

Read the artist's display name, website, and license from `artist.json` before
drafting. Replace all `[bracketed]` placeholders with real, specific content.

## Core structure

**Subject line:** Simple, honest, specific to their project.

```text
Free [genre] music for [Project Name]
Music for [Channel/Podcast Name] — Creative Commons licensed
[Genre] soundtrack for [Game/Film Name]
```

Not: "AMAZING OPPORTUNITY!!!" / generic / clickbait

**Opening (1–2 sentences)**

Introduce the artist and demonstrate genuine knowledge of the prospect's work.
Name something specific — an episode, a video, a scene, a mechanic.

```text
Hi [Name],

I'm [Artist Display Name], a [brief description — 1 clause] based in [location].
I've been [listening to / watching / following] [Project Name] and [specific
thing you genuinely appreciated].
```

**Value proposition (2–3 sentences)**

Lead with how it helps them, framed for their specific use case.

```text
I noticed [observation about their current music situation]. I release all my
music under [license from artist.json], which means you're free to use it in
[their project type] without licensing fees or complicated contracts.
```

**Specifics (2–3 sentences)**

Name tracks. Give preview links. Be concrete.

```text
I think [Track Name] or [Track Name] from [Album Name] might work well for
[specific use case you observed]. You can preview everything at [artist website]
or on [platform from artist.json].
```

**Call to action (1 sentence)**

Low pressure. Easy to say no.

```text
If any of it fits, I'd be happy to suggest more tracks or answer questions
about the licensing. No pressure either way.
```

**Closing**

```text
[Warm closing],
[Artist Display Name]

[Artist website]
[One or two platform links from artist.json]
```

## Attribution wording

Derive from `artist.json → default_license`. Standard CC BY 4.0 attribution:

> Music by [Artist Display Name] ([artist website]) — Licensed under CC BY 4.0

Simpler version for show notes or credits:

> Music: [Artist Display Name] ([artist website])

## Worked examples by creator type

### Podcast

```text
Subject: Free ambient music for [Podcast Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Artist Display Name], a [description] based in [location]. I've been
listening to [Podcast Name] and really enjoyed your recent episode on
[specific topic] — particularly [one specific thing].

I noticed your episodes [don't currently use background music / use music
sparingly]. I release all my music under Creative Commons licensing, which
means you're free to use it in [Podcast Name] without fees or complicated
contracts.

I think tracks like "[Track Name]" or "[Track Name]" might fit your show's
[tone/mood]. You can preview everything at [artist website] or on
[platform].

If you're interested, I'd be happy to suggest specific tracks. No pressure
at all.

Best,
[Artist Display Name]
[artist website]
```

### YouTube creator

```text
Subject: [Genre] music for [Channel Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Artist Display Name], a [description]. I came across [Channel Name]
and [specific video you watched] — [one genuine observation].

I create [genre description] and release everything under Creative Commons,
which means you can use it in your videos without copyright concerns or
fees. [Track Name] or [Album Name] might work well for [specific video
context or series you noticed].

You can preview at [artist website]. If any of it fits your aesthetic,
you're welcome to use it — just credit "[Artist Display Name]" in your
description.

[Warm closing],
[Artist Display Name]
[artist website]
```

### Indie game developer

```text
Subject: [Genre] music for [Game Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Artist Display Name], a [description]. I saw your [devlog/page] for
[Game Name] and love [specific thing — art style, mechanic, atmosphere].

Based on the mood you're going for, I think my music might work as
background ambience. I release everything under Creative Commons (CC BY 4.0),
so you can use it in [Game Name] without licensing fees — just credit me.

[Track Name] or [Album Name] might fit [Game Name]'s [specific
atmosphere]. You can listen at [artist website].

If you're interested, happy to discuss how it might work with your project.

Best of luck with development,
[Artist Display Name]
[artist website]
```

### Documentary / short film

```text
Subject: Music for [Documentary Title]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Artist Display Name], a [description]. I read about [Documentary Title]
and [specific thing about it that caught your attention].

I create [genre] music that's often used in documentaries and contemplative
films. All my music is Creative Commons licensed, so you can use it in
[Documentary Title] without licensing fees — particularly useful for
independent productions.

[Album Name] might fit [the tone / mood / subject] you're working with.
You can preview at [artist website] or [platform].

If you're still in post-production and looking for music, I'd be happy to
help find tracks that work for specific scenes.

Best,
[Artist Display Name]
[artist website]
```

## Follow-up (first, 7–10 days)

Brief, non-pushy, adds something if possible.

```text
Hi [Name],

Following up on my email from [date] about using my Creative Commons music
in [Project].

I know you're probably busy — no worries if this isn't a fit. Just wanted
to make sure my message didn't get buried. [Optional: I released [something
new] since then that might also be relevant.]

If you're not interested, that's completely fine — just let me know and I
won't follow up again.

Best,
[Artist Display Name]
```

## Positive response reply

```text
Hi [Name],

Great to hear from you! Here are direct links to the tracks I mentioned:

[Track 1]: [Bandcamp link] | [Spotify link]
[Track 2]: [Bandcamp link] | [Spotify link]

For attribution, something like:
"Music by [Artist Display Name] ([artist website]) — Licensed under [license]"

Let me know if you need different formats, have questions about the
licensing, or want suggestions for other tracks.

[Warm closing],
[Artist Display Name]
```

## Relationship check-in (3–6 months after placement)

```text
Hi [Name],

Hope you're well! I released [new album/track] last month and thought of
[Project]. It has [brief description of sound or mood].

No pressure at all — just wanted you to hear it first in case anything
works for future [episodes/videos/projects].

How's [Project] going?

Best,
[Artist Display Name]
[artist website]
```

## What to avoid

**Phrases:**

- "I'm reaching out to…"
- "I think you'll love…"
- "Don't miss this opportunity…"
- "We should collaborate…" (too presumptuous in a first email)
- "I noticed you might need…" (presumptuous framing)

**Behaviours:**

- Sending before thoroughly researching the prospect
- Copying any email verbatim from this file
- Following up more than once without new information
- Attaching files without permission
- Using urgency or scarcity language
