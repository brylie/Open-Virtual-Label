# Expense Tracking Guide

Definitions, decision guidance, and review process for music career expenses.
For calculation formulas, see [calculation-methods.md](calculation-methods.md).

---

## Why track expenses

1. Understand true profitability (revenue minus expenses = net income)
2. Identify which investments pay off
3. Make informed budget decisions
4. Prepare for tax reporting
5. Cut what doesn't work; invest in what does

---

## Expense categories

### Software / VSTs

DAW software, virtual instruments, effects plugins, utility plugins, upgrades.

**Record:**

```text
Date:     [Purchase date]
Item:     [Name and version]
Vendor:   [Company]
Cost:     €XX.XX
Purpose:  [What it enables or improves]
Category: Software/VST
```

**Review:** Do you use it regularly? Did it improve output quality? Would you
buy it again?

---

### Subscriptions

Distribution services, cloud storage, website hosting, plugin subscriptions,
sample libraries.

**Record:**

```text
Date:     [When charged]
Service:  [Name]
Cycle:    Monthly / Annual
Cost:     €XX.XX
Purpose:  [What it provides]
Category: Subscriptions
```

**Review quarterly:** Still using it? Getting value equal to cost? Is there a
cheaper or free alternative?

**Warning signs:** Haven't used in 2+ months; original need no longer exists;
free alternative would work; "I'll use it eventually" thinking.

---

### Promotion

Social media ads, playlist pitching, press release services, email marketing.

**Record:**

```text
Date:         [Campaign start]
Platform:     [Where]
Cost:         €XX.XX
Duration:     [How long]
Goal:         [Specific measurable outcome]
Category:     Promotion
```

**Always record results:**

```text
Impressions:    [Number]
Clicks:         [Number]
Conversions:    [Followers, streams, or sales attributed]
Revenue:        €XX.XX (conservative estimate)
ROI:            Positive / Negative / Unclear
Repeat?:        Yes / No / Modify
Learning:       [Key insight]
```

**Guidance:** Start with the smallest possible test (€10–20). Measure
everything. Stop what doesn't work. Scale only what demonstrates positive ROI.

---

### Performance

Transport, appropriate clothing, venue hire, printed materials, equipment hire.

**Record:**

```text
Date:     [Performance date]
Venue:    [Name and location]
Type:     Transport / Clothing / Venue / Materials / Hire
Cost:     €XX.XX
Category: Performance
```

**Notes:** Transportation, clothing, and materials should be reusable.
Community venues (e.g. volunteer eldercare performances) have no hire cost —
track only out-of-pocket costs.

---

### Equipment

Instruments, audio interfaces, microphones, headphones, cables, repairs,
piano maintenance, backup drives.

**Record:**

```text
Date:      [Purchase or service date]
Item:      [Description]
Vendor:    [Where]
Cost:      €XX.XX
Reason:    [Why needed now]
Expected lifespan: [Years]
Category:  Equipment
```

**Before purchasing:** Is this a genuine need or an upgrade for its own sake?
Can current gear be repaired? Is software (not hardware) the actual
bottleneck?

---

### Distribution / Admin

Distribution fees, ISRC codes, copyright registration, domain registration,
PRO membership.

**Record:**

```text
Date:     [When paid]
Service:  [What for]
Cost:     €XX.XX
Billing:  One-time / Annual
Category: Administration
```

---

### Education (optional)

Courses, books, workshops directly related to music craft or business.

**Record:**

```text
Date:     [Purchase date]
Resource: [Course or book name]
Cost:     €XX.XX
Topic:    [What it teaches]
Category: Education
```

**Guidance:** Much education is free (YouTube, documentation). Buy only when
free resources are genuinely insufficient. Finish what you buy before buying
more.

## Decision heuristics

### Should I buy this software?

Ask:
1. What specific problem does it solve?
2. Can I solve this with what I already have?
3. Is there a free alternative?
4. Will I use it within two weeks?
5. Is this a genuine need or an impulse?

If Yes to 1 and 4, No to 2, 3, 5 → consider purchasing. Otherwise → wait
30 days and reassess.

### Should I continue this subscription?

If not used in the last 60 days, or if a free alternative is available →
cancel or downgrade.

### Should I run this promotion?

Start with the smallest possible test. Measure results clearly. Scale only
if ROI is positive.

### Should I buy this equipment?

Does current gear actually fail at something specific? Will this tangibly
improve output? Have you maxed out skill with current gear?

## Spending guidelines

**As a percentage of revenue:**

| Expense ratio | Interpretation |
| ------------- | -------------- |
| <30% | Excellent — high profit margin |
| 30–50% | Healthy — sustainable |
| 50–70% | Concerning — low profit |
| >70% | Unsustainable |

**Red flags:**

- "I'll use it eventually"
- "It's on sale" (if you didn't need it at full price)
- "It will fix my creative block"
- "It's only €X" (small amounts compound)
- Recurring subscription forgotten about
- Equipment upgrade when skill is the limitation

**Green lights:**

- Solves a specific, current problem
- Used within one week of purchase
- ROI is measurable
- Replaces failing equipment
- Tested and proven (for promotional spend)

---

## Quarterly expense review

1. Total last 3 months and breakdown by category
2. ROI assessment: what generated value, what didn't
3. Adjustments: cancel unused, reduce ineffective, continue what works
4. Next quarter budget: essential recurring + planned one-offs + buffer

---

## Tax notes

Keep records of all expenses (receipts, bank statements, spreadsheet) in
case business expenses become deductible. Potentially relevant: software and
tools used exclusively for music, marketing and promotion, professional
services, education directly related to music skills. Consult a local tax
professional for specifics.
