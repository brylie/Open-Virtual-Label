## Goal Categories

Use these categories when setting or reviewing goals. Prioritise 2–3 categories
at a time; don't pursue aggressive goals in all areas simultaneously.

### Audience Growth

**Examples:** Reach 500 Instagram followers by [date]; grow Spotify monthly
listeners to 200 by [date]; increase YouTube subscribers to 100 by [date].

**Tracking metrics:** Follower count by platform, growth rate
(month-over-month), engagement rate alongside growth, quality indicators
(comments, saves, shares).

### Revenue

**Examples:** Reach €X/month average by [date]; generate €X in Bandcamp sales
this quarter; increase streaming revenue by X% this year.

**Tracking metrics:** Total monthly revenue, revenue by source, rolling
3-month average, growth rate.

### Content Creation

**Examples:** Post X Instagram stories per month; upload X YouTube videos per
month; create X behind-the-scenes pieces per week.

**Tracking metrics:** Posts published, consistency (on-schedule rate), content
performance, time invested per piece.

### Music Production

**Examples:** Complete mixing for X tracks by [date]; record X improvisations
this quarter; master and release X albums this year.

**Tracking metrics:** Tracks completed (recorded / mixed / mastered), production
hours, quality standards met, adherence to release schedule.

### Licensing & Placement

**Examples:** Secure X licensing placements by [date]; send outreach to X
potential users per month; build relationships with X active creators.

**Tracking metrics:** Outreach sent, response rate, placements confirmed,
ongoing relationships.

### Performance

**Examples:** Perform at X venues per month; add X new venue this quarter;
develop a X-minute improvisation set.

**Tracking metrics:** Performances logged, venue diversity, audience response,
personal comfort and artistic growth.

## Goal Setting Process

### Step 1 — Vision alignment

Before setting specific goals, ask:
- What matters most right now?
- Which area needs most attention?
- What is realistic given current constraints (time, energy, budget)?
- Does this serve the artist's core mission?

### Step 2 — Choose focus areas

Select 1–3 categories as primary focus for this period. Label the rest as
maintenance or on-hold.

```text
Primary (most attention, daily/weekly action): [Category]
Secondary (steady progress, less frequent): [Category]
Maintenance (keep ticking over): [Category]
On hold (explicitly deferred): [Category]
```

### Step 3 — Write SMART goals

For each focus area, use the template below.

### Step 4 — Break into actions

For each goal, identify weekly tasks, required resources, and potential
obstacles with mitigations.

### Step 5 — Establish check-in rhythm

- Daily: 5-minute progress glance
- Weekly: 15-minute review and adjustment
- Monthly: Full goal review with Artist Manager
- Quarterly: Strategic assessment and planning

## Goal Review Questions

### Weekly

1. What did I accomplish toward my goals this week?
2. What obstacles did I encounter?
3. What are my priorities for next week?
4. Am I maintaining a sustainable pace?

### Monthly

1. Which goals did I achieve?
2. Which goals need adjustment?
3. What worked well?
4. What should I do differently?
5. Am I celebrating progress?

### Quarterly

1. Did I make meaningful progress on strategic themes?
2. Which activities had the highest impact?
3. What should I continue, stop, or start?
4. Are my goals still aligned with my artistic vision?

## Common Pitfalls

**Too many goals** — Spreading energy across too many goals at once. Fix:
focus on 2–3 primary goals per month; make progress on a few things rather
than minimal progress on many.

**Unrealistic timelines** — Setting deadlines that ignore real constraints
(day job, life, rest). Fix: build in buffer; factor in how many hours per week
are actually available.

**Vanity metrics** — Chasing numbers that don't reflect meaningful progress
(e.g. follower count without engagement). Fix: track engagement quality
alongside quantity; note qualitative wins alongside numbers.

**No flexibility** — Rigidly sticking to goals when circumstances change.
Fix: review and adjust monthly; revising a goal is not failure.

**Not celebrating progress** — Only focusing on what's incomplete. Fix:
explicitly call out wins at each check-in, even small ones.
